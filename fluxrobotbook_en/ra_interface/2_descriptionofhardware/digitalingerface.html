connection with the safety wiring

Pins 1 to 16 are digital outputs, the remaining pins 17-18, 19-20 and 21-22 are contact pairs, which are bridged with potential free normally opened contacts. For both of the pins 23 and 24 for the EMERGENCY STOP circuits of the TruBend, the match ing mating contacts pin 15 and pin 16 are located on the plug XS8. This means that two in-line normally opened contacts are available in the EMERGENCY STOP circuit

If pins are given the same name, then these are potential-free normally opened contacts (S) or contact pairs (K). The contact pairs are bridged potential-free, the normally opened contacts switch a signal potential-free.

The EMERGENCY STOP circuits of the robot XS8:17-18 and XS8:19-20 must be bridged potential-free from the robot. For both pins 15 and 16 for the EMERGENCY STOP circuits of the machine, the matching mating contacts pin 23 and pin 24 are located on plug XS7. The pins XS8:21 and XS8:22 are bridged by the machine if the robot has been activated using the key switch. This normally opened contact can be used to switch off the robot drives when the machine is operated manually. The pins XS8:1 to XS8:14 listed above are digital inputs.

The pins XS8:23 and XS8:24 make a power supply available form the machine. With pin XS8:23 all digital inputs of the machine are set to logical true with 24 V, while consumers which are controlled using the digital outputs of the machine have their zero potential on pin XS8:24.

There is no potential separation with the inputs and outputs of the machine. Therefore potential separation must be applied on the robot side. Due to the low current required, this can also be done with an optocoupler or also with small signal relays.

EMERGENCY STOP circuits The press brake uses two independent EMERGENCY STOP circuits. The robots therefore also make their EMERGENCY STOP signals available in two ways. These signals are potential-free normally closed contacts. The robot is connected to the EMERGENCY STOP circuit of the machine via the plug XS8 pin 17/18

and pin 19/20. For the robot, two potential-free normally closed contacts are also provided from the machine via the pins XS8:15- XS7:23 and XS8:16-XS7:24 for connection into the EMERGENCY STOP circuits of the robot.

Notes

■ The robot is always connected into the EMERGENCY STOP circuit of the machine.

■ If an EMERGENCY STOP has been triggered, all "EMERGENCY STOP" push-buttons must be unlocked. The robot first needs to withdraw its EMERGENCY STOP, so that this can be acknowledged on the machine.

■ Attention: The following applies regardless of the operating mode the press brake and the connected option are in.

If an Emergency STOP is sent to the press brake by the connected option (robot), any movement is prevented. This also means that freeing a trapped person, e.g. via the "Press beam UP"button is no longer possible.

The Integrator must enter this point in its operator’s manual and provide the user with a description of the freeing of a trapped person.

■ Attention: The following applies regardless of the operating mode the press brake and the connected option are in.

If the press brake is in an Emergency STOP (e.g. because an "Emergency STOP" push-button was pressed), this status is passed on without delay to the connected option (robot) via the Emergency STOP signals described in Table 10.2(see "Tab. 10-2", pg. 10‐10).

The connected option must NOT return the Emergency STOP to the origin (press brake), unless an Emergency STOP is in place at the same time for the option.

The press brake behaves as soon as an Emergency STOP of the connected option is active.

The press brake stops all movements, but does NOT send the Emergency STOP back to the connected option.

The zero position of the valves of the TruBend Series 5000 (B23) is checked at the upper dead point. If this zero position is not reached in the required time, then the safety control detects a valve error.

This can be acknowledged using the "PRESS BEAM UP" foot switch and operation can then continue on the TruBend. Error acknowledgement from the bending application takes place in all operating modes by pressing the <START> button once more.

A key switch with two switch positions is fitted on the rear of the electrical cabinet. This can be used to select whether work takes place with or without the robot.
