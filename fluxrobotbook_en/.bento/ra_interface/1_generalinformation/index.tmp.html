Depending on your needs there are several types of coupling

DigitalIO coupling

The simplest operation are via the 27 pin digital interface. The robot follows the state machine in the machine and operates without any syncronisation.

Advantages:

  • Very simple interface

Disadvantages

  • Robot needs to release the part before a bend and picks it up again after the bend. This creates a singnificant loss of productivity and process stablility

Loose syncronized coupling

If the robot holds the part with spring loaded vacuum cups the motion of the robot could follow the machine motion with low accuracy.

Advantages:

  • A very error tolerant setup with high prodcutivity

  • Bigger parts get already support from the suction frame

Disadvantages

  • Vacuum grippers only

Thight syncronized coupling with FluxRobot

In case a offline programming software like FluxRobot is used the motion of the machine and the robot can be precisely syncronized.

Advantages:

  • highest prodcutivity and best work piece support

  • low setup time on the robotic cell
