Fanuc Motion commands

Fanuc Motion commands

We use the following combinations:

  1. Joint motion in Joint coordinates

  2. Joint motion in Cartesian coordinates

  3. Linear motion in Cartesian coordinates

  4. Linear motion in Joint coordinates

    • All motions could be fine (= exact motion) or CNT100 (=smoothend contour)

Joint motion in Joint coordinates

  • Works always

  • Collision risk since it goes not in straight lines

The cartesian coordinates consider the fine adjustment of UserFrames on the machine

LS-Command J P[1] 100% FINE ; → defined in percent of maximum speed

Joint motion in Cartesian coordinates

Cartesian coordinates could be reached by several axis configurations and therefore always need to have the additional attributes F/N, U/D, T/B . The addribute L/R is not neeed with our robot

Linear motion in Cartesian coordinates

All linear motion bear the risk that the Fanuc control gets into a error state because

  • The axis winds up and goes in a limit error

  • The robot comes to close to a singularity

  • The motion is changing the configuration (NUT→FUT).

The cartesian coordinates consider the fine adjustment of UserFrames on the machine

LS-Command: L P[4:JawApproach] 2000mm/sec FINE ;→ defined in mm/sec

Linear motion in Joint coordinates

Is possible, reduces a the swings of the robot but has the risk of singularities and limit errors

Summary: > Singularitys are unforseeable for small changes: use joint motion with joint coordinates as much as possible
